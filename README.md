# EDiE Research Hub  
**Evolutionary Discovery Engineering (EDiE)**  

_Where meaning survives, even as intelligence evolves._

---

## Overview

This repository is the central research hub for **Evolutionary Discovery Engineering (EDiE)** ‚Äî  
a research paradigm that shifts engineering from **designing solutions** to **discovering them through evolutionary processes**.

EDiE does not attempt to fully control or interpret intelligent systems.  
Instead, it defines environments, constraints, and evaluation pressures under which algorithms evolve, survive, and are later **discovered** by humans.

This repository documents EDiE as a **living research program**, not a finished theory.

---

## Core Principle

> **Design environments, not solutions.**  
> **Let evolution search.**  
> **Humans decide meaning.**

EDiE does not evolve in chaos.  
It evolves within a universe that has laws.

## Research Tracks

EDiE currently consists of five tightly connected research tracks:

### QAT ‚Äî Quantization as Survival Strategy
Quantization is treated not as post-optimization, but as an **evolutionary survival mechanism** under constrained resources.

- Auto-generated quantization strategies
- Competition under fixed hardware limits
- Survival-based selection rather than accuracy preservation alone

### WHY ‚Äî Iterative Why Robustness (IWR)
WHY introduces **IWR (Iterative Why Robustness)** ‚Äî a metric that measures semantic stability under repeated causal questioning.

- Explicit failure taxonomy

### AXI ‚Äî Evolving Evaluation Axes
AXI explores a radical extension of EDiE:

> **Evaluation criteria themselves should evolve.**

Instead of fixing benchmarks, AXI generates, competes, and selects **evaluation axes** based on their usefulness, robustness, and insight yield.

IWR currently serves as the first concrete axis candidate.

## Why Manual First?

Automation will come later.

EDiE intentionally begins with:
- human-executable protocols
- boring tools (Excel, logs)
- explicit rules and failure definitions

If a metric cannot be reproduced by another human,  
it is not yet a research contribution.
